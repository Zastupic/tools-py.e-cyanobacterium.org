Analysis of rapid light curves
(Photosynthesis-Irradiance curves)



Welcome to the application for analyzing rapid light curves (Photosynthesis-Irradiance, P-I curves)! Rapid light curves provide a detailed information on the ability of the studied organism to cope with increasing light intensity, on the overall performance and saturation level of photosynthetic electron transport rate as well as on the efficiency of both photochemical and non-photochemical quenching. The rapid light curves can be measured for all photosyntheetically active organisms, i.e. for plants, algae, cyanobacteria or photosynthetic bacteria.

This tool visualizes and analyzes rapid ligth curves curves, as obtained by PAM fluorometry measurements. The tool reads up to 50 rapid light curve files and plots several graphs: raw fluorescence signal and parameters Ft, Fm, qP, qN, NPQ, Qy and ETR (see the summary table below).
Besides plotting the fluorescence signals and calculating these parameters under each light intensity, the curves are fitted by a model described by Platt et al. (1980):

$$ \begin{multline} \shoveleft rETR = rETR_{mPot} (1 - e^\frac{\alpha PAR}{rETR_{mPot}}) e^{-\frac{\beta PAR}{rETR_{mPot}}}, \end{multline} $$

This allows to derive additional paramters such as photosynthesis efficiency under low irradiance (α), photoinhibition coefficient under high irradiance (β), maximum potential electron transport rate (ETRmax), or irradiance required to reach maximal rETR in theoretical scenario where photoinhibition would be absent. The full list of parameters is summarized below:

Parameter Formula Unit Meaning
Technical parameters
PAR µmolphotons m-2 s-1 Photosynthetically active radiation
Ft, Fm' Steady-state and maximum fluorescence intensity under each tested PAR level, respectively
Fm' (max) Maximum fluorescence intensity through all tested PAR levels
 
Efficiencies, quantum yields and quenching
Qy (Fm' - Ft) / Fm' Effective quantum yield of electron transport through PSII
rETR Qy * PAR µmolelectrons m-2 s-1 Relative electron transport rate; assuming that the value of photons absorptance by PSII is unknown
qP (Fm' - Ft) / (Fm' - F0) Coefficient of photochemical quenching (flow of the captured light energy into photochemical reactions)
qN (Fm' (max) - Fm') / (Fm' (max) - F0) Coefficient of non-photochemical quenching (flow of the captured light energy into heat)
NPQ (Fm' (max) - Fm') / Fm' Non-photochemical quenching (flow of the captured light energy into heat)
 
Parameters based on the light curves fitting
α electrons photons-1 Initial slope of the light curve, related to quantum efficiency of photosynthesis
β electrons photons-1 Slope of the light curve under the highest intensities, related to photoinhibition
ETRmPot µmolelectrons m-2 s-1 Potential maximum ETR without photoinhibition considered
ETRmax ETR [α / (α + β)] [β / (α + β)](β/α) µmolelectrons m-2 s-1 Maximum electron transport rate
Ik ETRmax / α µmolphotons m-2 s-1 Irradiance required to saturate ETRmax
Ib ETRmax / β µmolphotons m-2 s-1 Irradiance required to photoinhibit ETRmPot, also known as photoinhibition index
