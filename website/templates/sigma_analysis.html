Sigma(II) Analyzer Functional Absorption Cross-Section of PSII

At a glance: Upload .csv files exported from the Multi-Color PAM fluorometer (Heinz Walz GmbH) to visualize the wavelength-dependent functional absorption cross-section of photosystem II [σ(II)λ]. Compare samples with interactive spectra and per-wavelength scatter plots, assign replicates to groups for mean ± SD statistics, and export results to .xlsx.

The functional absorption cross-section of PSII, σ(II)λ (nm²), describes the effective light-harvesting area of a single PSII reaction centre at a given excitation wavelength λ. It integrates both the physical antenna size and the quantum efficiency of energy transfer to the reaction centre, providing a wavelength-resolved picture of how efficiently each PSII unit collects light. Measurements are performed simultaneously at five excitation wavelengths (440, 480, 540, 590 and 625 nm) using a brief single-turnover flash, from which the O–I₁ fluorescence rise kinetics are recorded and fitted.

Representative σ(II) spectrum or O–I₁ rise kinetics.
Figure: Schreiber et al. (2012) Photosynth Res 113:127–144.

The Multi-Color PAM (Walz GmbH) uses five actinic LEDs (440, 480, 540, 590, 625 nm) to measure σ(II)λ at each wavelength using the Sigma1000 protocol. For each wavelength, a series of single-turnover actinic flashes is applied to the sample while recording the fast O–I₁ fluorescence rise.

The kinetics are fitted by the reversible radical pair (RRP) model (Schatz et al. 1988; Lavergne & Trissl 1995), yielding τ (time constant of QA reduction) and connectivity parameter p, from which σ(II)λ = k(II) / (L · PARλ) is calculated. Results are exported via WinControl-3 as a semicolon-delimited CSV.

Walz GmbH (2015) Multi-Color-PAM, Photosynthesis Yield Analyzer — Handbook of Operation. Heinz Walz GmbH, Effeltrich, Germany.
