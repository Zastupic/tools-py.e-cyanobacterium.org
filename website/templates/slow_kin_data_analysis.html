Slow kinetics data analysis


Welcome to the application analyzing slow fluorescence transients! These fluorescence transients, so-called Kautsky kinetics, or NPQ protocols, provide a sensitive method for evaluating the efficiency of electron (e-) transport within the photosynthetic electron transport chain.

Parameter Formula Alternative formula Meaning
Technical parameters
F0 Minimum fluorescence intensity in dark-adapted state
Fm Maximum fluorescence intensity in dark-adapted state, measured during the first saturation flash after dark adaptation
Fm' Maximum fluorescence intensities during the course of slow kinetics measurement
Fm' (max) Maximum fluorescence intensity throughout the course of slow kinetics measurement
Ft Steady-state fluorescence intensities during the course of slow kinetics measurement
Ft (L) Steady-state fluorescence intensities in the light-acclimated state
Fp Steady-state fluorescence intensity in the peak of fast Kautsky induction
PAR Photosynthetically active radiation (units: µmolphotons m-2 s-1)
Fv Fm - F0, Fm' - Ft Variable fluorescence intensities during the course of slow kinetics measurement
 
Efficiencies, quantum yields and quenching
Fv / Fm (Fm - F0) / Fm Maximum quantum yield of primary PSII photochemistry, measured in dark-adapted state
Qy (Fm' - Ft) / Fm' Effective quantum yield of PSII during the course of slow kinetics measurement
rETR Qy * PAR Relative electron transport rate during the course of slow kinetics measurement
NPQ (Fm - Fm') / Fm' (Fm' (max) - Fm') / Fm' Non-photochemical quenching during the course of slow kinetics measurement
qN (Fm - Fm') / Fm (Fm' (max) - Fm') / Fm' (max) Coefficient of non-photochemical quenching
qP (Fm' - Ft) / (Fm' - F0) Coefficient of photochemical quenching, an estimate of open PSII reaction centers

How to use this tool:

  1. Select your fluorometer
  2. Upload and analyze slow kinetic files (up to 50 files)
  3. Review the results
  4. Download the results in a summary .xlsx file

Accepted file formats include .CSV and .txt files, as obtained by MULTI-COLOR-PAM / DUAL-PAM or AquaPen / FluorPen fluorometers, respecitvely:

MULTI-COLOR-PAM DUAL-PAM AquaPen FluorPen







Please note that MULTI-COLOR PAM files typically contain large data and may take longer time to process. For faster processing, it is recommended to reduce size of the MULTI-COLOR PAM files. This will also allow to export reduced data to the summary .xlsx file for further processing. Reduced files will contain about 10000 data points.
If you wish to work with raw data, uncheck the box below. For AquaPen / PlantPen, data reduction is not necessary.
Please note that it is only possible to work with one file type at a time, although up to 50 files can be processed within a single analysis. The AquaPen/FluorPen files have identical length, due to defined NPQ protocols by the manufacturer. For the MULTI-COLOR PAM files, it is recommended to process only files measured by identical protocols, as defined by the user/experimenter.

For the MULTI-COLOR PAM files with parameters, following timing is considered for F0 and Fm
F0 0 s (the first measured Ft point)
Fm 0 s (the first measured Fm point)

3. For the MULTI-COLOR PAM files, select time points to fit Fm' kinetics

The Fm' data can be interpolated by exponential function, in order to estimate rate of state transition. Protocols in AquaPen or PlantPen are pre-defined, and timing of dark and actinic light periods is fixed. On the other hand, protocols in Multi-Color PAM or Dual PAM are user-defined, and timing of dark and actinic light periods is variable.
Below, timing of individual periods within the slow kinetic protocol from Multi-Color PAM or Dual PAM can be specified, in order to evaluate state transitions rate also in protocols with variable timing.
