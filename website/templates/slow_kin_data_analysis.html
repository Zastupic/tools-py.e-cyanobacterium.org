Analysis of slow chlorophyll fluorescence transients

At a glance: Upload PAM fluorometer files from NPQ / slow kinetics protocols to visualize and quantify time-dependent fluorescence changes — including non-photochemical quenching (NPQ), state transitions, photochemical quenching (qP), and quantum yield. No software installation needed; results download as a formatted .xlsx file. Supports batch processing of up to 50 files.
About this tool

This tool enables visualization and analysis of fluorescence traces acquired by several common PAM fluorometers, including MULTI-COLOR-PAM, DUAL-PAM, AquaPen and FluorPen. It is well suited for the analysis of NPQ induction and relaxation, ETR efficiency, state transitions, and other long-term fluorescence responses of cyanobacteria, algae, and higher plants.

  2. For AquaPen/FluorPen: choose the NPQ protocol used during the measurement
  3. For MC-PAM/Dual PAM: choose whether you are uploading raw data or parameter files
  4. Upload up to 50 files and click Upload & Analyze
  5. Explore traces, derived parameters, and group statistics in the interactive tabs
  6. Optionally assign files to groups to compare replicates with mean ± SD
  7. Download results as .xlsx; export scalar parameters to the Statistics page

Accepted file formats: .csv (MC-PAM / Dual PAM) and .txt (AquaPen / FluorPen, with parameter export enabled).
