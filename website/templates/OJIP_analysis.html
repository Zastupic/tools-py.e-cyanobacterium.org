Analysis of fast fluorescence transients (OJIP)


Welcome to the app for OJIP files analysis!
To analyze your OJIP files, please follow the instructions below:

  1. Select your fluorometer
  2. Upload OJIP files exported from your fluorometer (up to 50 files)
  3. View OJIP curves and calculated parameters
  4. Download the processed result as .xlsx file

Imporant notice:
All files are required to have identical number of measured points (identical length).

Please note that MULTI-COLOR PAM files typically contain large data and may take longer time to process. For faster processing, it is recommended to reduce size of the MULTI-COLOR PAM files. This will also allow to export reduced data for further processing. Reduces OJIP curves will contain about two thousand data points (data between F0 and FI are unabridged).
If you wish to work with raw data, uncheck the box below.
For AquaPen / PlantPen, data reduction is not necessary.

List of parameters (for references, see Stirbet et al., 2018 and Tsimilli-Michael 2020):
Parameters Formula Meaning
F0, FP Minimal and peak fluorescence values, respectively
FK,FJ, FI Fluorescence values measured at 300 μs, 2 ms and 30 ms, respectively
VJ,VI Vt = (Ft – F0) / (FP – F0) Relative variable fluorescence at time (t) of J and I points, respectively
M0 4(F0.3 ms - F0.05 ms) / (FP – F0) Initial slope (in ms–1) of the O-J fluorescence rise
ψE0 1 - VJ Efficiency with which a PSII trapped electron is transferred from QA- to PQ
ψR0 1 - VI Efficiency with which a PSII trapped electron is transferred to final PSI acceptors
δR0 ψE0 / ψR0 Efficiency with which an electron from PQH2 is transferred to final PSI acceptors
φP0 (FP - F0) / FP Maximum quantum yield of primary PSII photochemistry
φE0 φP0 x ψE0 Quantum yield of electron transport from QA- to PQ
φR0 φP0 x ψR0 Quantum yield of electron transport from QA- to final PSI acceptors
ABS/RC (M0 / VJ) / φP0 Apparent antenna size of an active PSII
TR0/RC M0 / VJ Maximum trapped exciton flux per active PSII
ET0/RC M0 / ψE0 The flux of electrons transferred from QA- to PQ per active PSII
RE0/RC M0 / ψR0 The flux of electrons transferred from QA- to final PSI acceptors per active PSII
DI0/RC ABS/RC - TR0/RC The flux of energy dissipated in processes other than trapping per active PSII
J-I slope (FI - FJ) / 28 Slope (in ms–1) of the J-I fluorescence rise
