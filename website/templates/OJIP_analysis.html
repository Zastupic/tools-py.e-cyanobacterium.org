OJIP Analyzer: Online Chlorophyll Fluorescence Transient Analysis

At a glance: Upload raw data files from your PAM fluorometer to instantly visualize OJIP fluorescence curves and calculate photosystem II efficiency parameters — including Fv/Fm, PIabs, and the full JIP-test parameter set. Supports batch processing of up to 100 files with interactive charts, replicate grouping, and export to .xlsx.

Welcome to the tool analyzing fast chlorophyll fluorescence induction kinetics! Fast chlorophyll fluorescence induction kinetics, known also as OJIP transient, provides a rapid and sensitive method for assessing the efficiency of electron (e-) transport within the photosynthetic electron transport chain. Analysis of the OJIP transient allows to determine bottlenecks in the e- transport chain at various sites — including charge separation at photosystem II (PSII), the plastoquinon (PQ) pool, or at the site of photosystem I (PSI).

Polynomial inflection point identification (Akinyemi et al. 2023)

The fluorescence signal in each window is fitted with a 9th-degree polynomial. Inflection points (▲) are identified as roots of the 2nd derivative where the 3rd derivative is positive. The inflection closest to the expected timing is assigned as FJ or FI.

FJ identification — 9th-degree poly 2nd derivative, O-J window (0.5–5 ms); inflection points (▲) where d²=0, d³>0
FI identification — 9th-degree poly 2nd derivative, J-I window (10–100 ms); inflection points (▲) where d²=0, d³>0

How to interpret your results (for plants and algae)
  • Fv/Fm — max PSII efficiency. Unstressed: 0.75–0.82; mild stress: 0.60–0.74; severe: <0.55.
  • ABS/RC — effective antenna per active RC. Values >3–4 may indicate photoinhibition.
  • ET₀/RC and TR₀/RC — electron transport and energy trapping per RC. Declining relative to ABS/RC indicates downstream blockage.
  • ψE₀ — efficiency of QA−→PQ electron transfer; decreases with PQ pool over-reduction.
