Fluorescence Spectra & EEM Analyzer

At a glance: Upload 3D excitation-emission fluorescence maps measured at 77 K or room temperature to visualize pigment-protein complex composition and calculate PSII/PSI ratios and phycobilisome coupling states. Supports batch processing of up to 100 files with interactive charts, replicate grouping, and export to .xlsx.

Excitation-emission matrix (EEM) fluorescence spectroscopy enables highly sensitive assessment of content, ratios, and interactions of pigment-protein complexes — photosystems (PSI, PSII), phycobilisomes (PBS), and their accessory pigments. At 77 K, biochemical modulation of fluorescence is suppressed, allowing clear discrimination of spectral signatures from individual complexes — essential for assessing stress responses, physiological adaptation, or genetic diversity in cyanobacteria, algae, and higher plants. Room-temperature (RT) EEM measurements provide complementary information on PBS coupling states and PSII:PSI ratios under physiologically relevant conditions.

  1. Select your spectrofluorometer, upload up to 50 .csv files, and click Analyze
  2. Spectra tab — adjust excitation/emission wavelengths to plot and normalization mode; click Re-extract spectra to update without re-uploading
  3. 2D Map tab — view full EEM maps for all samples; select colorscale and optionally crop the display range
  4. Derived Parameters tab — select analysis mode (77K / RT) and pigmentation type; parameters update instantly
  5. Groups tab — assign samples to groups to compute mean ± SD and compare conditions
  6. Download results as .xlsx or export scalar parameters to the Statistics page

Accepted file formats: .csv (Jasco FP-8050/8550 or AMINCO-Bowman Series 2 Jasco-compatible export) and .spc (Horiba FluoroMax-P, Galactic SPC K-format — upload all files per sample together; files with the same name prefix are combined into one EEM).

Analysis mode:
Low-temperature EEM — photosystem stoichiometry & PBS coupling
Pigmentation:
77K mode: Ex440/Em689 (Chl-PSII) and Ex440/Em724 (Chl-PSI) report PSII and PSI chlorophyll fluorescence; their ratio is the primary PSII/PSI stoichiometry indicator. PBS excitation bands (Ex560 for PE, Ex620 for PC) report PBS coupling state: PBS-PSII/PBS-PSI ratio reflects State 1 (PBS coupled to PSII) vs State 2 (PBS coupled to PSI). Absolute thresholds are species- and condition-specific.
RT mode: PBS-PSII norm and PBS-PSI norm (fractions of total PBS signal at Ex620) are the primary state-transition indicators. F685/F730 (Ex440) reports the overall PSII:PSI Chl ratio. Absolute thresholds are species- and condition-specific.
